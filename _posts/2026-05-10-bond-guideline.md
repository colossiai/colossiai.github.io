# US Bond investment guideline

In **Futu (Moomoo)**, you can buy US short-term bonds in two main ways:

1. **Direct US Treasury bonds (individual bonds)**
2. **US Treasury ETFs (short-term bond funds)** ← *most popular for retail investors*

---

# 🇺🇸 1. How to buy in Futu (step-by-step)

Inside the Futu / Moomoo app:

1. Go to **Discover → Wealth**
2. Tap **Bonds**
3. Choose **US Treasury / US Bonds**
4. You can filter by:

   * maturity (e.g. < 1 year, 1–3 years)
   * yield (YTM)
5. Select a bond or ETF
6. Tap **Buy**, enter amount (usually min ~USD 1,000 for bonds)
7. Confirm order

👉 This is consistent with Futu’s bond flow and US Treasury module. ([Futu香港][1])

---

# 🧠 2. Easiest way: US short-term bond ETFs (recommended)

If you just want **simple “cash-like US T-bill exposure”**, ETFs are much easier than individual bonds.

## 📌 Popular US short-term Treasury ETFs

### 🟢 Ultra short (cash alternative, < 3 months)

* iShares 0-3 Month Treasury Bond ETF (SGOV)
* Very close to US T-bill rates
* Low volatility
* Highly liquid

### 🟢 Short-term Treasury

* iShares Short Treasury Bond ETF (SHV)

* Holds Treasury bills < 1 year

* Extremely stable price

* Vanguard Short-Term Treasury ETF (VGSH)

* Slightly higher duration → slightly more yield

* iShares 1-3 Year Treasury Bond ETF (SHY)

* Moderate short-term exposure

---

# 💡 3. Which one should you choose?

### If your goal is:

### 🟢 “Park cash safely like FD / MMF”

→ Use **SGOV / SHV**

### 🟡 “Slightly higher yield, still low risk”

→ Use **SHY / VGSH**

### 🔵 “Simple long-term allocation”

→ Mix short + medium Treasury ETFs (bond ladder style)

---

# ⚠️ 4. Important notes (Futu users often miss this)

* You are usually buying **ETFs, not real bonds**
* ETF price fluctuates slightly (but low risk)
* Currency is **USD exposure → FX risk (SGD moves matter)**
* Bond buying in Futu may have:

  * spread cost
  * minimum ticket size (~USD 1,000 for direct bonds)
