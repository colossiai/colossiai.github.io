Explain what is HBM and why it's important to AI infra

**HBM** stands for **High Bandwidth Memory**.
It is a special type of ultra-fast memory used mainly in AI accelerators and high-end GPUs such as the NVIDIA H100/B200, AMD MI300, and AI chips from Google TPU systems.

---

# Simple idea

Think of AI chips as giant factories.

* **Compute cores** = workers
* **Memory** = warehouse supplying materials

Modern AI chips can perform enormous amounts of computation, but they become useless if data cannot reach them fast enough.

HBM solves this by delivering **massive memory bandwidth**.

---

# Why normal RAM is not enough

Traditional server RAM (DDR4/DDR5):

* good capacity
* relatively cheaper
* farther away from CPU/GPU
* lower bandwidth
* higher latency

AI training needs to constantly move:

* model weights
* activations
* gradients
* KV cache
* tensors

This movement is enormous.

For large AI models, memory bandwidth often becomes the bottleneck instead of raw compute.

---

# What makes HBM different

HBM stacks DRAM vertically using **3D packaging** and connects it to the GPU/chip using an extremely wide bus.

Instead of:

* few narrow roads (traditional RAM)

HBM provides:

* gigantic multi-lane highways

---

# Visual concept

Traditional memory:

```text
CPU/GPU ---- narrow bus ---- RAM
```

HBM:

```text
GPU
 ├── HBM stack
 ├── HBM stack
 ├── HBM stack
```

Memory sits physically very close to the compute die.

---

# Key advantages of HBM

## 1. Extremely high bandwidth

Example rough numbers:

| Memory Type | Bandwidth    |
| ----------- | ------------ |
| DDR5        | ~50–100 GB/s |
| GDDR6       | ~1 TB/s      |
| HBM3e       | 4–5+ TB/s    |

This is critical for transformer workloads.

---

## 2. Better power efficiency

Moving data is expensive in power.

HBM reduces:

* distance
* signaling cost
* energy per transferred bit

This matters because AI datacenters are increasingly power-limited.

---

## 3. Enables giant AI models

Large models require:

* huge parameter storage
* large batch sizes
* massive tensor movement

Without HBM:

* GPUs would stall waiting for memory
* tensor cores become underutilized

HBM is one reason modern LLM training became feasible.

---

# Why HBM is strategically important

HBM is now one of the biggest bottlenecks in the entire AI supply chain.

AI demand depends not only on GPUs, but also:

* HBM manufacturing
* advanced packaging
* CoWoS capacity
* memory yields

This is why companies like:

* SK hynix
* Samsung Electronics
* Micron Technology

became critically important in AI infrastructure.

---

# AI infra impact

## Training

HBM allows:

* faster throughput
* larger models
* larger context windows
* higher GPU utilization

Training clusters scale much better with high-bandwidth memory.

---

## Inference

Inference increasingly depends on HBM too because:

* KV cache grows with context length
* multi-user serving needs huge memory bandwidth
* reasoning models require more token compute

Long-context LLMs are heavily memory-bound.

---

# Example: why GPUs are expensive

A large portion of modern AI GPU cost comes from:

* HBM stacks
* advanced packaging
* silicon interposer technology

In many cases, HBM availability limits GPU production more than the GPU chip itself.

---

# Important AI infra trend

The industry is shifting from:

```text
Compute-centric
```

to:

```text
Memory-bandwidth-centric
```

This is why newer AI chips emphasize:

* more HBM stacks
* higher HBM generations
* memory-centric architectures
