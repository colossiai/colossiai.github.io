**What is the most important and practical stock evaluation criteria for individual investor in stock trading?**

For most individual investors, the **most practical stock evaluation criteria** is:

# **Quality Growth at a Reasonable Price**

Not the “cheapest stock.”
Not the “fastest growing stock.”
But a business with:

1. **Strong and durable growth**
2. **Healthy financials**
3. **Reasonable valuation**
4. **Understandable business model**

That is how many successful long-term investors operate.

---

# The 5 Most Important Criteria (Practical Priority Order)

## 1. Revenue & Earnings Growth (MOST IMPORTANT)

A stock usually rises long term because the **business grows**.

Look for:

* Revenue growing consistently
* EPS (earnings per share) growing
* Free cash flow growing

Good signs:

* Revenue growth > 10–15%
* EPS growth > 15%
* Growth sustained for multiple years

Why it matters:

* Growth drives future valuation
* Institutions buy growing companies

Example:

* NVIDIA exploded because AI demand caused massive earnings growth.

---

# 2. Competitive Advantage (“Moat”)

Ask:

> Why can competitors NOT easily destroy this company?

Strong moat examples:

* Brand
* Network effect
* Ecosystem lock-in
* High switching costs
* Proprietary technology

Examples:

* Apple → ecosystem lock-in
* Microsoft → enterprise dominance
* ASML → near-monopoly EUV machines

This is extremely important because:

* Weak businesses eventually lose margins
* Great businesses compound for decades

---

# 3. Valuation (Don’t Overpay)

Even amazing companies can be bad investments if overpriced.

The most practical valuation metrics:

| Metric    | Best For                    | Practical Meaning          |
| --------- | --------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| PE        | Stable profitable companies | How expensive earnings are |
| PEG       | Growth stocks               | PE adjusted for growth     |
| EV/EBITDA | Mature businesses           | Operating valuation        |
| P/S       | Unprofitable growth stocks  | Revenue valuation          |

---

# The MOST Useful Metric for Individual Investors:

# PEG Ratio

Formula:

```text
PEG = PE / Earnings Growth Rate
```

Interpretation:

| PEG | Meaning                 |
| --- | ----------------------- |
| < 1 | Potentially undervalued |
| 1–2 | Fair                    |
| > 2 | Expensive               |

Why PEG is practical:

* Combines growth + valuation
* Easier than DCF
* Very useful for tech stocks

Example:
A stock:

* PE = 30
* Earnings growth = 30%

PEG = 1

That can actually be reasonable.

---

# 4. Financial Strength

Avoid companies that can die during downturns.

Check:

* Debt level
* Cash reserves
* Free cash flow
* Profit margins

Simple practical checks:

* Positive free cash flow
* Debt not exploding
* Gross margin stable or improving

Excellent businesses often have:

* High margins
* Strong balance sheet
* Large cash position

Examples:

* Meta Platforms
* Alphabet

---

# 5. Industry Trend (“Tailwind”)

Even average companies can rise in strong industries.

Strong long-term tailwinds today:

* AI infrastructure
* Cloud computing
* Cybersecurity
* Semiconductor equipment
* Robotics
* Energy infrastructure

Examples:

* Broadcom
* Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
* CrowdStrike

Professionals usually prioritize:

```text
Business Quality
→ Growth
→ Margins
→ Competitive moat
→ Management
→ Valuation
```

NOT:

* Candlestick patterns
* Random indicators
* “Hot tips”
* Meme hype

# Practical “Fast Checklist” for Retail Investors

Before buying a stock, ask:

| Question                           | Desired Answer |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------- |
| Is revenue growing?                | Yes            |
| Is EPS growing?                    | Yes            |
| Is the industry growing?           | Yes            |
| Does it have a moat?               | Yes            |
| Is debt manageable?                | Yes            |
| Is valuation reasonable?           | Yes            |
| Can I explain the business simply? | Yes            |

If most answers are “yes,” it’s worth deeper research.

# Best Overall Strategy for Most Individuals

For long-term success:

## Focus on:

* High-quality companies
* Strong secular growth
* Reasonable valuation
* Long holding periods

## Avoid:

* Overtrading
* Penny stocks
* Pure speculation
* Buying only because price fell
